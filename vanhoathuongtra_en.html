Dai Tu tea drinking culture

1. Tea in People's Lives

The tea drinking culture of Thai Nguyen people in general, and Dai Tu people in particular, revolves around connecting people with people, with nature, and with the motherland. The art of tea enjoyment goes deep into everyone's heart, becoming an indispensable part of daily life.

Locals cherish the value of this cultural beauty by using tea as a means of communication, creating bonds, and bringing a sense of peace and relaxation.

"Gradually replacing the traditional 'betel nut starts the conversation', 'a cup of tea marks the beginning of a story', continuing to demonstrate respect and politeness in communication culture."

Enjoying tea requires a relaxed, quiet state of mind to fully perceive the exquisite flavor of the tea. Letting go of worries to enjoy tea helps life slow down and become lighter.

2. The Art of Tea Enjoyment

Every ethnic group and region has its own way of enjoying tea. Vietnamese people summarize this art through the saying: "First Water, Second Tea, Third Cup, Fourth Pot, Fifth Friends".

First Water (Nhất Thủy)

Water is the most important factor. Ancients used spring water or dew on lotus leaves. Today, pure water can be used.

Second Tea (Nhì Trà)

Select tea based on standards: clarity, color, taste, and spirit. Tea suitable for preference and good quality ensures the standard flavor.

Third Cup (Tam Bôi)

"Three brewings" refers to the brewing method. Tea drinkers need to know the basic steps to awaken the best tea flavor.

Fourth Pot (Tứ Ấm)

Depending on the drinking style "solitary, pair, or group", choose appropriate pots and cups to help retain the original flavor.

Fifth Friends (Ngũ Quần Anh)

Good tea cannot lack good friends. Finding a tea soulmate (confidant) to enjoy together is the most precious thing.

3. Standard Tea Brewing Process

To create a fragrant and standard cup of Thai Nguyen tea, meticulous steps are required:

Step 1
Warm the Pot

Rinse the pot and cups with 90°C boiling water and discard. Helps clean and retain heat.

Step 2
Measure Tea

Put a sufficient amount of dry tea (about 1/5 of the pot) into the warmed pot.

Step 3
Awaken Tea

Pour water just covering the tea and discard immediately. Helps clean dust and start leaf expansion.

Step 4
Brew Tea

Pour 80-85°C boiling water. Steep for about 1-2 minutes depending on preferred strength.

Step 5
Pour & Enjoy

Pour tea evenly into cups (circular pouring). Enjoy while hot.

4. Meaning of Vietnamese Tea Culture

The tea enjoyment culture of Vietnamese people has long been
developed based on the precious traditional values of the nation.

Vietnamese tea culture evokes stories shared over a cup of tea. We set aside elaborate rules to whisper and sip tea together in a simple yet poetic setting.

Freshly poured tea has a fresh yellow-green color, and the tea scent fills the room. When tasting, behind that astringent bitterness is the perception of hardship and meticulousness in the labor process to create delicious tea. The sweet aftertaste that lingers is a metaphor for the soul of our people: valuing gratitude, faithfulness, and loyalty.
